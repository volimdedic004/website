BYESON. A pure high school passion project. Eighteen tracks, eighteen years of my perspectives, struggles, emotions, love, ambition, and growing up. Nothing's perfect about this raw and unmastered album (the vocals were recorded on my iPhone 15) and that is the beauty of it. Don't expect much, approach with low expectations, and don't take it personally.

## **Behind the Magic**

The summary above is what I posted on my music Instagram page @dariusthepianoboy on September 22nd, 2025. This album was my first attempt at making high enough quality studio music that would meet the standards I set for myself to put it up on streaming. Needless to say, I was pretty spot on with saying that the album has its flaws, espeically regarding the unmastered vocals that can easily overwhlem parts of the tracks.

The origins of BYESON started while I was still performing original songs at Carlisle High School's Coffeehouse shows and won the Horizons Grant in January 2024. During that time, I created the track "DRAGONS" for what would become my scholarship project itself. A full 3:06 track with oringinal instrumentals, vocals, lyrics, and production. I ended up performing this track in the following year's January performance. Prior to this, I had also made the tracks "LIGHT BLITZ" and "LETTIN' IT GO," alongside a track that never made the final album "LOVE CARLISLE." It was after making these first few tracks that a Elias Kradel, a close friend of mine, said I should make an album with what I currently have. I saw this as a perfect opportunity to both show the world that foundation of what I can do with music and to keep myself busy with making even more original tracks.

When it came to brainstorming ideas for each individual track, most came with time of observation, as BYESON draws heavily off of my life experiences during my senior year at Carlisle High School. Taking it literally into the heart and soul of Cumberland Valley, I had two of my friends drive me out during August 2025 into the middle of a cornfield to take the photo that became the album cover for BYESON.

## **BYESON's Tracklist**

### 1 - **EXPOSITION**

**"You want to start your life? You wanna live big and dream big?"**
A simple introductionary skit that sets the scene of where I want the story of this album to go. I had a Zack Haney, a current class of 2026 senior, record the lines of the announcer who encourages me to "give a speech" about a reflection of my life before it really begins to inspire others to look back too. This track really has a reference to Kanye West's "Intro" track in his debut album "The College Dropout" with how I wanted Zack to approach his lines.

### 2 - **COMMENCEMENT**

**"This my Commencement."**
Kinda a fun diss track poking fun at little references within our class that probably burnt some bridges and that some probably took personally. Inspired from my graduation ceremony. At the time, me and many other fellow musicians were surprised, and honestly kinda annoyed, that three out of four of our commencement speakers essentially had the same sport-oriented attributes, so I decided to write my frustrations into a song. I have very different opinions now looking back and honestly could care less now about who spoke during the ceremony, but now I see it more as a capsule of my prior grudge that I do not really agree with anymore. It samples "Fireflies" by Gato Barbieri and "Bewitched (Are You Leaving Soon)" by Naomi Akimoto at the end. I would like to particularly mention the presence of light in this track, as the four commencement speakers during our ceremony also wrote around the theme of light, so I decided to add an extension to that theme by representing the 370 (369 now unfortunately) students as those who want to seek the light of success post-graduation.

### 3 - **URBAN**

**That my Aerospace Teacher was a pedo-----**
During my sophomore year, I had an physics teacher who taught Aerospace Engineering (who shall not be named, if you know you know) who was just about forced to quit after allegations of engaging in unprofessional and inappropriate conversations with a bunch of the girls in his classes. This happened about a third of the way into the schoolyear, meaning that most of my time in sophomore year we were in a very rough space with no qualified teacher to help us (shoutout to the substitute who had to deal with us all those months). While there were few good memories with the people I was with, we honestly hated it, and this song serves as a reminder that I once had an aerospace teacher who was a pedophile. This track samples the drums of "Make You Better" by Gramatik, "Matrenrou Monogatari" by Noriko Miyamoto in the chorus, and "Silly Crush" by Masaki Matsubara in the verses.
Two fun facts about this track:
- Three people who were in this aerospace class are in jail now, I'll let you decide who one of them is.
- Current teachers reached out to me months after BYESON's release to say that this was their favorite track

### 4 - **BIKLO (INTERLUDE)**
**Biklooooooooo**
A simple interlude that transitions from URBAN to BIKLO. I really wanted to see how far I could go with my music abilities without much lyrical input, I'd say I did a pretty good job at that.

### 5 - **BIKLO**
**What comes next for me and Biklo I can't say, least it made a hella good college essay**
A track based off my bycicle I named "Biklo," who ended up becoming a prominent figure in my Common Essay for college applications (as I mention in my other post in this website). I highly recommend checking out that other article because I go much more in depth with the meaning behind my lyrics (because they heavily pull from the essay itself), but essentially the story is pretty self explanatory and I wanted to shed light on the bike that helped me get into George Washington University. I still bike this year from Mount Vernon campus to Foggy Bottom every single day, and I plan on taking Biklo on a 100-mile bike ride this upcoming Summer 2026.

### 6 - **DEPENDENCY**
**It's hard to love someone that doesn't love you back**
A little song on an observation I noticed throughout my time at CHS. It always kinda bothered me at the time that it felt like a lot of people constantly felt the need to date someone and to always have someone there for them; that aspect of being with someone is not a bad thing of course, rather how would these people handle themselves if they weren't dependent on someone else to keep them happy. I personally just think managaging to keep yourself happy in isolation is just as important so that when you do eventually find someone it only gets better. Because of this observation, I wrote a song about metaphorically struggling to get myself heard by someone I would be into, or trying to show interest in someone so I can do something like a homecoming proposal, but the person I like doesn't reciprocate or is into someone else, it would hurt. This is personally one of my favorite tracks to play on an acoustic piano because it's so different from the others.

### 7 - **LETTIN' IT GO**
**I just thought I would let you know, I'm lettin' it go**
This song combines two elements: moving on from relationships, and moving on from Celsius energy drinks, only one of which I have successfully done (Celsius keeps me SANE). I intentionally put this track after "DEPENDENCY" to serve as a transition from a "oh I got rejected" to a "screw it" mentality, because essentially the person of interest and a can of Celsius can be treated the same in this track when trying to let either one go in my life. This track has a very quick sample of "Demolition Inevitable" which is a track from the game "Five Nights At Freddy's: Sister Location"; something to note is that this track plays when it is revealed that "Springtrap," a rotting corpse inside an animatronic suit, is still alive, which I used as a metaphor of how I too would rot if I were to continue my Celsius addiction. This song explains itself pretty well with overcoming addiction and all, so much so that I ended up performing it at the January 2025 Coffeehouse show with people only really understanding the Celsius perspective. I just wanted to clarify that it is NOT the only perspective of this song and I kept it hidden during the performance.

### 8 - **LIGHT BLITZ**
**You can make something you hate into something great**
No complicated backstory, no relationship struggles, just simple song about waking up the morning. I used to hate waking up at 6:05AM every morning to get to CHS on time, it was honestly the only part of high school I really did not enjoy, so making a song about it was my best way of coping at the time. Notice the light reference once more, this was the first original track I performed at Coffeehouse and the first track I wrote lyrics for PERIOD, so it really served as the baseline for all my other tracks to connect the light theme together. I mention the phrase "Soy Ploy" in this track, which refers to the name of the first band I performed with in Coffeehouse that really got me into it in the first place. I also released this track as my first single before releasing BYESON (the cover is at a cabin a couple close friends and I stayed at during a trip after graduation in Vermont).

### 9 - **SUMMER TWILIGHT**
**Don't know what life's about but I need to figure out**
My third and final track about relationships, this one in particular about a breakup. I actually wrote this track the same night it happened as I was still out on vacation in the Outer Banks, and it served as a reflection for my current self to also look back on and see who I was and how much I've taken out of previous mistakes to do better. The "Twilight" in this case is just a representation of downward decline, and a desire to do better and be better out of the experience. This was my second and final single before releasing BYESON (the cover is of the shore of the Atlantic at Outer Banks where I wrote the lyrics).

![SummerTwilight](/website/assets/img/SummerTwilight.png){: .mx-auto.d-block :}

### 10 - **BOOMIN''20S**
**Proved boomin' '20s was really a scam**
This track really comes from the quote "they said we would have flying cars by now," or the thought of having people by Mars by now. Really just an overall critique of the first few years of the 2020s on how some parts of what we were promised was slowed so much to the COVID-19 pandemic and other elements. I also talk about my progression in those years too, with how much more responsbility I have in my life now over before and how my personality has evolved. A lot of people like this track despite it probably being the simplest and shortest for me to make (other than lyrical content).

### 11 - **BLUEBERRY JUICE**
**You wanted a song, so I will give you one**
My personal favorite track on BYESON. I wanted to give a little homage to Coffeehouse as a thanks for inspiring me to produce and create BYESON, and I thought it would not be a Coffeehouse track without as many Cofeehouse perfomers within it as possible. The whole reason for the title "Blueberry Juice" is kinda niche, but it comes from how whenever I had bandmates over for practices, we would always have *Fructal Classic* blueberry juice that I introduced to them. The track starts off with a direct transition from "BOOMIN''20S" with the phrase "Guess no one cares but I just wanna | LIVE," with the | transitioning songs, so it does sound kinda weird just jumping straight into the song without listening to the prior one. Kai Lesman plays trumpet at the start and end of the song, Chase Perabo features as a primary artist with an entire verse and Elias Kradel (yes, the same Elias who encouraged me to make an album) has background vocals later in the track, alongside some other people who also provide minor background vocals. If I had a track that I would constantly revisit, it would be this one.

### 12 - **DRAGONS**
**Ambition is a Dragon too**
The song that really sparked my ambition for starting to make what would become BYESON, as this was the first song I produced. It really follows the idea that ambition is a great thing to follow, but it can also become overwhelming very quickly and lead to burnouts. That mostly came from the fact that I was in at least a dozen clubs during my senior year, so a burnout was inevitable. This track samples Kanye West's unreleased (and hopefully will never release) song "Never See Me Again" in the chorus, the guitar riff from "Where The Light Is" by Surfaces, and "menu-in-game version" by nicopatty during the bridge. That bridge is what inspired the whole dragon theme, because it really did feel like a Dungeons and Dragons fantasy theme that my bandmates noticed, so much so that we performed this song under the alias "D&D" (Daris & the Dragons). When first producing BYESON, I honestly considering naming the album "DRAGONS," and the cover was going to be an illustration of a depressed dragon sipping on Celsius, but that ultimately faded over time with all the other tracks.

![DnD](/website/assets/img/DnD.JPEG){: .mx-auto.d-block :}

### 13 - **EXTROVERSION**
**Still feel alone, but you already know**
I made up the word "Extroversion" for this song, but essentially it is another track specifically about my transition from an introverted personality throughout elementary and middle school to around my sophomore year where I began joining clubs and getting myself involved with more people, building a new "Extroversion" of myself. I do mention in this track that I'm "still alone," but it serves more of a reminder of who I used to be. This track kinda has a rough production especially during the final part and honestly in my opinion, is kind of a skip. It samples the chorus structure of "Vivir Mi Vida" by Marc Anthony, mostly cause I was thinking about my Costa Rica trip I did with my class during the summer of 2024 while making this track. I see that trip as one of the true transition points from introvert to extrovert, and I do not plan on going back.

### 14 - **FREE-TIME**
**Don't know if its worth living? Just try its the key**
This track was made with the prediction that I would lack the free-time during college to make more music, and for the most part that has been true; it also references all the time I put into doing scholarship applications, thus the lack of free-time to really enjoy going out with people and enjoying final moments for the time. A very straightforward track with really good production, it just lacks the vocals (they are ROUGH) I hoped for and I rushed it for sure. I included a quote at the end: "We are the music makers, And we are the dreamers of the dreams" from Arthur O'Shaughnessy mostly just because I thought it fit the idea of the power of musicians with ideology.

### 15 - **TRAPPED**
**Stuck with this heritage, whether I like it or not**
I wanted to make a track that focused on my Bosnian roots, and this was my attempt at doing so. I reference the Sebrenica Genocide and some aspects of growing up with Bosnian culture and living up to what my family had to go through, but I think the delivery could had been better since this track has very little lyrics, and when there are lyrics they are performed and mastered ROUGHLY. I also include a semi-poorly implemented clock chime at the end that was supposed to be a sample of my grandparent's wedding clock, but I was never able to get that original audio into the track and it still annoys me to this day. I may redo the idea of this track in the future because I honestly consider it to be the worst track on BYESON.

### 16 - **BYESON**
**BYE-SON, you stand out of crowds made mommy proud**
The self-titled track contains references to all fifteen prior tracks, with the meaning of this song being the literal meaning of the album. If the name BYESON was not obvious enough, it serves as my departure from Carlisle High School (who's mascot is a Bison). The composition was actually made long before I even had the idea of BYESON during my time directing our high school's Shakespeare Pit Band, where the full composition was the final track we played in the Fall 2024 show. Then, funny enough, this was the last song to get lyrics on BYESON, meaning that I spent more time making this track than any other. The production on this track is my second favorite on the album, besides "BLUEBERRY JUICE," and I love how I was able to splice up all the lyrics to make a new meaning out of them.

### 17 - **GRADUATION**
**But we'll be back...**
I originally made this song only with an acoustic piano part and my vocals just to perform at my Senior Renaissance show, but decided to make a full studio version and add it to BYESON after a lot of people praised it. Just one final goodbye and final reflection that I love to look back on as I performed this the day before my graduation ceremony. Yes, someone did vandalize the bison statue outside our high school; and yes, I did actually state my phone number during the live performance, but I had to remove it for obvious reasons.

![Graduation](/website/assets/img/Graduation.JPEG){: .mx-auto.d-block :}

### 18 - **RESOLUTION**
I had Zack Haney do vocals for both this track and "EXPOSITION" at the same time, because if you loop these two skits together they transition perfectly together; I released the instrumental version where I start it with this track so this can be heard. I see this track as a new beginning to a new life, but that new life requires a reflection of the old which is exactly why I loop it back to the start.

BYESON is definitely one of those one-time listen albums that I would personally never blast in a car or play while I work, but it serves as a nice permanent capsule of my high school life for me to grow off of. Nevertheless, I am proud of the nine months I put into creating this 66 minute long album, and I do not intend on stopping here, cause we are just getting started.
