BYESON. A pure high school passion project. Eighteen tracks, eighteen years of my perspectives, struggles, emotions, love, ambition, and growing up. Nothing's perfect about this raw and unmastered album (the vocals were recorded on my iPhone 15) and that is the beauty of it. Don't expect much, approach with low expectations, and don't take it personally.

## **Behind the Magic**

The summary above is what I posted on my music Instagram page @dariusthepianoboy on September 22nd, 2025. This album was my first attempt at making high enough quality studio music that would meet the standards I set for myself to put it up on streaming. Needless to say, I was pretty spot on with saying that the album has its flaws, espeically regarding the unmastered vocals that can easily overwhlem parts of the tracks. Nevertheless, I am proud of the nine months I put into creating this 66 minute long album.

The origins of BYESON started while I was still performing original songs at Coffeehouse and won the Horizons Grant in January 2024. During that time, I created the track "DRAGONS" for what would become my scholarship project itself. A full 3:06 track with oringinal instrumentals, vocals, lyrics, and production. I ended up performing this track in the following year's January performance. Prior to this, I had also made the tracks "LIGHT BLITZ" and "LETTIN' IT GO," alongside a track that never made the final album "LOVE CARLISLE." It was after making these first few tracks that a Elias Kradel, a close friend of mine, said I should make an album with what I currently have. I saw this as a perfect opportunity to both show the world that foundation of what I can do with music and to keep myself busy with making even more original tracks.

When it came to brainstorming ideas for each individual track, most came with time of observation, as BYESON draws heavily off of my life experiences during my senior year at Carlisle High School. Taking it literally into the heart and soul of Cumberland Valley, I had two of my friends drive me out during August 2025 into the middle of a cornfield to take the photo that became the album cover for BYESON.

## **BYESON's Tracklist**

### 1 - **EXPOSITION**

**"You want to start your life? You wanna live big and dream big?"**
A simple introductionary skit that sets the scene of where I want the story of this album to go. I had a Zack Haney, a current class of 2026 senior, record the lines of the announcer who encourages me to "give a speech" about a reflection of my life before it really begins to inspire others to look back too. This track really has a reference to Kanye West's "Intro" track in his debut album "The College Dropout" with how I wanted Zack to approach his lines.

### 2 - **COMMENCEMENT**

**"This my Commencement."**
Kinda a fun diss track poking fun at little references within our class that probably burnt some bridges and that some probably took personally. Inspired from my graduation ceremony. At the time, me and many other fellow musicians were surprised, and honestly kinda annoyed, that three out of four of our commencement speakers essentially had the same sport-oriented attributes, so I decided to write my frustrations into a song. I have very different opinions now looking back and honestly could care less now about who spoke during the ceremony, but now I see it more as a capsule of my prior grudge. It samples "Fireflies" by Gato Barbieri and "Bewitched (Are You Leaving Soon)" by Naomi Akimoto at the end. I would like to particularly mention the presence of light in this track, as the four commencement speakers during our ceremony also wrote around the theme of light, so I decided to add an extension to that theme by representing the 370 (369 now unfortunately) students as those who want to seek the light of success post-graduation.

### 3 - **URBAN**

**That my Aerospace Teacher was a pedo-----**
During my sophomore year, I had an physics teacher who taught Aerospace Engineering (who shall not be named, if you know you know) who was just about forced to quit after allegations of engaging in unprofessional and inappropriate conversations with a bunch of the girls in his classes. This happened about a third of the way into the schoolyear, meaning that most of my time in sophomore year we were in a very rough space with no qualified teacher to help us (shoutout to the substitute who had to deal with us all those months). While there were few good memories with the people I was with, we honestly hated it, and this song serves as a reminder that I once had an aerospace teacher who was a pedophile. This track samples the drums of "Make You Better" by Gramatik, "Matrenrou Monogatari" by Noriko Miyamoto in the chorus, and "Silly Crush" by Masaki Matsubara in the verses.
Two fun facts about this track:
- Three people who were in this aerospace class are in jail now, I'll let you decide who one of them is.
- Current teachers reached out to me months after BYESON's release to say that this was their favorite track

### 4 - **BIKLO (INTERLUDE)**
**Biklooooooooo**
A simple interlude that transitions from URBAN to BIKLO. I really wanted to see how far I could go with my music abilities without much lyrical input, I'd say I did a pretty good job at that.
